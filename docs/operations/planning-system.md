## 1. Philosophy

**Primary rule:** document decisions and outcomes, not activity.
If it takes more than 2 minutes to log, it will not get logged consistently.
If nobody reads it, it should not exist.

**Core lemma:** Speed through Divide and Conquer.
Every goal is decomposed into the smallest identifiable subproblem that is independently solvable. No task exists that does not trace up to a yearly goal.

```
Yearly goal
    └── Quarterly OKR  (×4, 13-week sprints)
            └── Monthly Milestone  (×3 per quarter)
                    └── Weekly Sprint Goal  (×4-5 per month)
                            └── Daily Task  (EOD log entry)
```

**What is logged:** decisions, outcomes, goal progress, meeting outputs, financial closes.
**What is NOT logged:** brainstorms, debugging threads, daily standups, soft decisions reversed within a week.

**Oriol owns all documentation.** Arnau and Adam never touch Notion structure. Oriol extracts from them in the weekly sync and does the write-up. Their cognitive resources go entirely to building and selling.

## 3. OKRs vs KPIs

### KPIs — permanent health dials

Answer: *is the business healthy right now?*
They never disappear. Measured continuously. Read every week.

```
ARR, pipeline count, A score, churn rate, burn rate, runway
```

### OKRs — temporary directional targets

Answer: *where are we trying to go this quarter?*
They expire at quarter end. Hit or missed, then replaced.

```
Objective:    Qualitative, inspirational, directional.
              "Prove XC Gradient has product-market fit in industrial SMEs"

Key Results:  Quantitative, measurable, binary at deadline.
              KR1: 2 paying PoCs signed by Jun 30
              KR2: A score > 0.82 on Decfa corpus
              KR3: Pipeline of 5 qualified leads active
```

**The relationship:**
```
OKR  →  defines the destination
KPI  →  tells you if you're moving toward it

If your OKR is "2 paying clients by Q2 end",
your KPI "pipeline conversations/week" is the early warning system.
```

**Rule:** if all KRs are green, the Objective is achieved. If you can hit all KRs without achieving the Objective, your KRs are wrong.

**Why OKRs over KPIs alone:** KPIs tell you the business is healthy but give no direction. You could have great KPIs and be building the wrong thing. OKRs force a quarterly answer to "what matters most right now?"

## 4. The Growth Model

### North Star: ARR = N_clients × ACV

Two levers, always tracked separately:

```
N growth:    new PoC → pilot → paid conversion
ACV growth:  existing client buys new module (plugin upsell)
```

The weekly question is never "did we grow?" — it is always one of:
**"Did we add a client or did we upsell an existing one?"**

### The Compounding Math

```
Annual target:     ARR × 3     (+300%)
Quarterly target:  ARR × 1.75  (+75% per Q, compounds to ×3 annually)
Weekly target:     ARR × 1.06  (+6% WoW, compounds to ×1.75 per Q)

Good:   5–7% WoW growth
Magic:  10% WoW growth

At 10% WoW → 5× in a year
At 5% WoW  → 12× in a year (compounding)
```

### Pre-revenue north star (Phases 0–1)

Before the first paying client, ARR is meaningless. North star becomes:
**conversations with potential customers per week.**
The WoW growth target applies to pipeline conversations, not revenue.

## 5. Planning Cadence

Top-down cascading: you can only define a smaller goal correctly once the bigger goal it serves is defined. Never the reverse.

```
WHEN                    WHAT YOU DEFINE                    HORIZON

Late Dec / early Jan    Annual goal                        12 months
  └── Same session      Q1 OKRs (fully detailed)           3 months
      └── Same session  Jan, Feb, Mar milestones            1 month each
                        Q2/Q3/Q4 are direction stubs only

End of each quarter     Next quarter OKRs (fully detailed)  3 months
  └── Same session      3 monthly milestones                1 month each

End of each month       Next month goals refined            4 weeks

End of each week        Next week sprint card               5 days

End of each day         Tomorrow's 3 priorities             1 day
```

**Key discipline:** Q3 and Q4 are always stubs until you get there. Detailing Q4 in January is wasted planning — the world will have changed.
